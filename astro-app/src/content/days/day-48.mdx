SAEs decompose these polysemantic neurons into “monosemantic features” — individual, interpretable concepts like “the Golden Gate Bridge,” “deception,” or “code syntax.” This is groundbreaking because for the first time, researchers can identify specific, interpretable concepts inside large models and understand how those concepts influence outputs.
